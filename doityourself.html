DO-IT-YOURSELF-DEMOCRACY

What does the do-it-yourself-democracy look like compared to a traditional democracy?

Our current democracy knows many problems: fragmentation, low turnouts at elections, political deadlock and an increasingly widening gap between the citizen and the government. Even with good spirit, it is extremely hard to solve these problems within the existing political system, so why would we not rise to the challenge ourselves? When we observe a concrete problem, for instance a loose pavement tile, we could wait for some form of government to solve it, but we can also take matters into our own hands! And when we do so, we do it as a collective actor instead of a loose collection of individuals: that is how do-it-yourself-democracy should work!

Tips for do-it-yourself-democracy

  • Do not only think about your own interests, but try to look at how you can improve a situation for all the inhabitants of your neighborhood/municipality!
  • Organize! Make sure that, when you want to reach a certain goal, you team up with a group.
  • Accept that opinions might differ between you and other people, but make sure that you can always reach a compromise: unity in diversity!
  • Take into account existing inequalities and be creative when it comes to solving those: when people cannot come to a town hall meeting because they have children at home, it might be worth the effort to arrange a nanny!
  • Try to convince people within the system (civil servants, members of the municipality council) that there is more than the system world they are living in: oftentimes it is more a lack of awareness than a lack of good will!
  • Take a look at what organizations already exist that try to solve problems within the neighborhood or municipality: it is unnecessary to double the workload!
